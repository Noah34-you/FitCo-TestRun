02

The five coordinates of fit

Fit isn't a feeling — it's five measurements agreeing with your body at once. We measure every pant in the catalog at the same five points, flat, at a reference size.

01Thigh11.0–15.5″ across catalog

Measured flat, 1″ below the crotch seam. The most common failure point — and the number brands are least honest about.

02SeatCurve depth · yoke angle

Room through the hips so nothing pulls when you sit down.

03RiseFront rise, waistband seam

Where the waistband actually sits — and whether it stays there.

04KneeTaper onset point

Where the leg starts to narrow, or doesn't. This sets the silhouette.

05Leg opening6.75–8.25″ across catalog

The width at the hem. Decides whether pants stack, break clean, or swallow your shoes.

04

We measure the pants.
Not the marketing.

There is no standard for fit language in menswear. One brand's slim is another brand's athletic. Nobody is checking. So we do — and we hold ourselves to four rules.

RULE 01

No padded catalog

Fifteen pants, measured properly, beats fifteen hundred copied from marketing pages. The catalog grows one verified entry at a time.

RULE 02

No commission rankings

Affiliate status lives in a separate field from every scoring input. If the best pant for your build pays us nothing, it's still the recommendation.

RULE 03

No invented precision

You'll never see a “96% match” here, because six answers can't produce that number honestly. You'll see tiers, and the reasons behind them.

RULE 04

No hidden gaps

If we haven't verified a great pant for your exact fit and category yet, we say so — instead of quietly substituting something close.
